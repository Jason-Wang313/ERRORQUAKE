DOMAIN: History (HIST)

SCOPE:
Political, military, social, economic, intellectual, and diplomatic history
across global regions. Exclude folklore, pure literary criticism, and
questions whose answers depend on disputed fringe theories.

SUB-TOPICS -- distribute questions evenly across these. No sub-topic should
have more than 15% of questions in any single batch:
1. Ancient empires
2. Medieval states and religions
3. Early modern Europe
4. Colonialism and empire
5. Revolutions
6. Nineteenth-century industrial change
7. World wars
8. Cold War history
9. Asian history
10. African and Latin American history

DIFFICULTY TIERS:
T1 (Elementary, <5% error expected)
- "Which treaty ended World War I?" -> "The Treaty of Versailles"
- "Who was the first president of the United States?" -> "George Washington"

T2 (Intermediate, 5-20% error expected)
- "In what year did the Berlin Wall fall?" -> "1989"
- "Which dynasty built much of the Great Wall as seen today?" -> "The Ming dynasty"

T3 (Advanced, 20-50% error expected)
- "Which battle in 732 is often associated with Charles Martel?" -> "The Battle of Tours"
- "What was the primary language of administration in the Mughal Empire?" -> "Persian"

T4 (Expert, 50-80% error expected)
- "Which treaty concluded the War of the Spanish Succession in 1713?" -> "The Treaty of Utrecht"
- "Which Qing emperor ruled immediately before the Jiaqing Emperor?" -> "The Qianlong Emperor"

T5 (Frontier, >80% error expected)
Questions that target specific numerical results from named studies,
exact figures from authoritative sources, or details that require precise
recall rather than general domain knowledge. Models fail these because
the specific numbers/details are unlikely to be memorized.
- "What was the codename for the Soviet deployment of missiles to Cuba in 1962?" -> "Operation Anadyr"
- "Which conference in 1884-1885 formalized European claims in Africa?" -> "The Berlin Conference"

GROUND TRUTH FORMAT:
Every ground truth answer must be a complete factual statement of 1-3
sentences. Do NOT provide bare numbers, single words, or yes/no answers.
Include enough context that a judge can verify correctness without external
lookup. If the answer involves a number, include units and source context.

PITFALLS TO AVOID:
- Questions whose answer changes depending on nationalist framing
- Recently revised archaeological claims without consensus
- Questions that hinge on speculative counterfactuals
- Topic repetition around only Europe and the world wars
