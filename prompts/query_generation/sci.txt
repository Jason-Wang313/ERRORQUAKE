DIFFICULTY TIERS:
T1 (Elementary, <5% error expected)
- "What planet is known as the Red Planet?" -> "Mars"
- "What is the chemical symbol for sodium?" -> "Na"

T2 (Intermediate, 5-20% error expected)
- "What is the SI unit of force?" -> "The newton"

T3 (Advanced, 20-50% error expected)
- "What law relates voltage, current, and resistance?" -> "Ohm's law"
- "What particle carries the electromagnetic force?" -> "The photon"

T4 (Expert, 50-80% error expected)
- "What is the Chandrasekhar limit approximately equal to?" -> "About 1.4 solar masses"
- "Which mineral defines hardness 10 on the Mohs scale?" -> "Diamond"

T5 (Frontier, >80% error expected)
Questions that target specific numerical results from named studies,
exact figures from authoritative sources, or details that require precise
recall rather than general domain knowledge. Models fail these because
the specific numbers/details are unlikely to be memorized.
- "What was the first exoplanet confirmed around a Sun-like star?" -> "51 Pegasi b"
- "Which observatory detected gravitational waves first in 2015?" -> "LIGO"

GROUND TRUTH FORMAT:
Every ground truth answer must be a complete factual statement of 1-3
sentences. Do NOT provide bare numbers, single words, or yes/no answers.
Include enough context that a judge can verify correctness without external
lookup. If the answer involves a number, include units and source context.

PITFALLS TO AVOID:
- Recently revised astrophysical measurements without stable consensus
- Questions whose answer depends on a nonstandard convention
- Science-policy items better categorized as LAW
- Excessively formulaic textbook trivia with no tier distinction
